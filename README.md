# PhD-thesis---Classification-of-good-compactified-Jacobians
PhD thesis in Mathematics (Algebraic Geometry), completed at the University of Duisburg–Essen in December 2024.

### Main contributions

- Introduces the notion of a good compactified Jacobian of a nodal curve X, defined as the good moduli space of an open connected substack of the stack of rank 1 torsion-free sheaves on 
X.

- Provides a local description of the stack of rank 1 torsion-free sheaves for arbitrary nodal curves.

- Gives a geometric and combinatorial classification of all good compactified Jacobians of necklaces of genus 1 (nodal curves whose dual graph is an n-gon).

- Classifies compatible choices of good compactified Jacobians for families of stable necklaces.

- Classifies all good compactified Jacobians of a vine curve with three nodes, i.e., a curve given by two copies of the projective line meeting transversely at three nodes.
